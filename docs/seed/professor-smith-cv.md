## Research program (summary for students & collaborators)

The **Smith Laboratory for Translational Immuno-Oncology** studies how the **tumor microenvironment** reshapes **myeloid** and **T cell** function in solid malignancies, with emphasis on **biomarkers** that predict response to **immune checkpoint blockade** and combination strategies. We integrate **mouse models**, **human biospecimens** (de-identified; IRB-approved banking protocols), **multiplex immunoassays**, and **computational** analysis of high-parameter cytometry.

**Representative themes**

- Myeloid-derived suppressor cells & macrophage polarization in post-therapy niches  
- Co-evolution of **exhausted CD8+ T cell** phenotypes with stromal remodeling  
- Pre-analytical variables in **ELISA / Luminex** workflows for translational cohorts  
- Open, reproducible pipelines (**R**, **Python**) for cytometry QC and visualization

**Funding (selected)**  
NIH R01 (MPI, contact PI), U01 consortium subaward, DOD CDMRP Idea Award, foundation pilot grants.

## Mentorship philosophy

Dr. Smith trains **undergraduate**, **MSTP**, and **postdoctoral** scientists in a **team-based** culture: weekly 1:1s, structured journal clubs, explicit training in experimental design, figure preparation, and **IRB/IACUC** literacy. Undergraduates routinely co-author posters and contribute to manuscripts when they meet authorship criteria. Many mentees have gone on to **MD**, **MD/PhD**, and **PhD** programs.

**Ideal undergraduate researcher profile**

- Strong grounding in **molecular biology**, **cell culture**, and/or **immunology** coursework  
- Interest in **pre-med** / physician-scientist pathways and **translational** questions  
- Comfort with **flow cytometry** or willingness to complete intensive training  
- Basic **R** or **Python** for plotting & reproducibility (or commitment to learn)  
- Professional communication, reliability, and collaborative mindset
